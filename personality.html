AI for Travel and Hospitality

My favourite idea from the AI Businesses of 2025 is the use of AI for travel and hospitality.
This idea uses artificial intelligence to turn social media travel videos from Tik Tok and Instagram into bookable itineraries.
I find this fascinating because it connects creativity, social media, and technology in a practical way.

Application in the Real World

As a student and traveler, I could use an app like this to plan local trips across Jamaica. As well as summer vacations when school gives holiday.
Instead of manually searching for directions and hotels, the AI could create a travel plan based on videos I've saved or liked.

This could also benefit small businesses such as guest houses, restaurants, and tour guides by turning their social media content into real bookings.
For education, it could help plan study trips, cultural exchanges, and field trips.

Why It Would Be Useful

  • Time-saving - AI can instantly create travel plans from content you already enjoy.
  • Personalised - Recommendations are based on your interestts and budget.
  • Promotes local tourism - Encourages visitors to explore smaller, less-known destinations.
  • Supports small businesses - Local creators can showcase unique experiences through content.

Ethical Issues and Solutions

Ethical Concern Possible Problem Suggestion Solution
Data Privacy AI systems may collect user data without clear consent. Use transparent privacy policies and allow users to control what data is shared.
Bias Popular destinations may be over-represented, ignoring local or diverse voices. Train AI on diverse data and include user feedback to ensure fairness.
Over-Automation AI may ignore cultural details or promote over-tourism. Combine AI automation with local human input and cultural awareness.

Example App in This Area

One real-world example is GuideGeek by Matador Network, an AI-powered travel assistant that helps users plan trips through chat platforms like WhatsApp and Instagram.
